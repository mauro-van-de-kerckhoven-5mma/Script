About Us

In April 1994, Supreme opened its doors on Lafayette Street in downtown Manhattan and became the center of New York skate culture. The heart of the store consisted of a group of neighborhood kids, New York skaters, and local artists who joined the store's staff and customers. Supreme became a symbol of downtown culture and played a crucial role in its constant renewal. Skaters, punks, hip-hop fans and young counterculture all gravitated toward Supreme. As it became an institutional center of the city, Supreme established itself as a brand known for its quality, style, and authenticity. In 25 years, Supreme has grown from its New York roots into a global community and worked with generations of artists, photographers, designers, musicians, filmmakers, and writers who defied conventional boundaries and contributed to its unique identity and attitude.
